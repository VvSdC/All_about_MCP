### 🧠 Core Concepts of MCP

The Model Context Protocol (MCP) acts like a **negotiation table**. When an AI (Host) connects to a data source (Server), they perform a "handshake" to agree on what they can do together. This prevents "fragile" code—one server works for any AI that speaks MCP.

* **Resources (The Library):** Read-only data. Think of it as the AI looking at a document.
* **Prompts (The Recipe):** Pre-written instructions. Like a "Summary Template" the AI can follow.
* **Tools (The Power Tool):** Actions. This gives the AI "hands" to delete files or send emails.
* **Sampling (Phone-a-Friend):** When the server asks the AI, "Hey, I'm stuck, can you explain this error for me?"
* **Roots (The Fence):** Safety boundaries. You tell the AI, "You can work in the '/Project' folder, but stay out of my '/Photos'."
* **Elicitation (The Question):** When the AI clarifies things: "I found two files named 'Draft', which one should I use?"
* **Transports (The Wire):** The physical connection. **STDIO** is like a direct cable on your PC; **SSE** is like a Wi-Fi connection to the web.

### 🔄 The Conversation Flow

Think of the communication sequence like a formal meeting:
1.  **Host (The Boss):** "I need to talk to the Google Drive server."
2.  **Client (The Assistant):** Starts the meeting and handles the handshake.
3.  **Server (The Specialist):** "I'm ready. I can 'read_files' and 'list_folders'. What do you need?"

### 🖥️ Hosts vs. 🔌 Clients

* **The Host:** The "Main App" (e.g., Claude Desktop, VS Code). It owns the UI and manages your security settings.
* **The Client:** The "Connector" inside the Host. It handles the specific 1:1 "talk" with a server.

> **Real-World Analogy:** The **Host** is your iPhone. The **Clients** are the individual apps (like Spotify or Instagram) that connect to their specific **Servers** to get your music or photos.

---

### 🧠 The Context Object (`ctx`)
In Python, the `Context` object is like a **Security Badge**. It gives your tool extra info about the current session and allows it to report back to the "Boss" (the Host).

'# Example: Using Context to log what's happening'
'@mcp.tool()'
'def search_db(query: str, ctx: Context):'
'    ctx.info("Searching the database now...")'
'    return db.execute(query)'

### 🤖 The MCP Agent
An **MCP Agent** is an AI that has been given "hands." It doesn't just chat; it uses the protocol to solve problems across different apps automatically.

* **Dynamic Chaining:** The agent reads an error log (**Resource**), understands the bug, and then restarts the server (**Tool**) without you asking twice.
* **Requirement:** The AI must be smart enough to "Function Call" (like Gemini 1.5 Pro or Claude 3.5).

### ⚙️ The Three Primitives (Superpowers)

| Power | Who is in charge? | Real-World Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Prompts** | **You** (User-controlled) | You select a "Code Review" template from a menu. |
| **Resources** | **The App** (App-controlled) | The app automatically feeds the AI your current `.log` file. |
| **Tools** | **The AI** (Model-controlled) | The AI decides: "I need to search Google now to answer this." |
